Distance, Displacement, Speed & Velocity — Interactive Learning

Concept Overview

Distance is the total length of the path traveled, regardless of direction (scalar).

Displacement is the shortest straight-line change from start to end, including direction (vector).

Speed = Distance / Time, scalar; Velocity = Displacement / Time, vector.

Key Differences

Real-Life Examples

  • Jogging one lap on a track (400 m) → Distance = 400 m, Displacement = 0.
  • Driving to a store 5 km away and back → Distance = 10 km, Displacement = 0.
  • Speedometer shows speed, GPS shows velocity (with direction).
